About me

Hello, my name is Avary Hales. I am a 21-year-old student from Boise, Idaho, currently studying pre-business at Brigham Young University. Growing up, athletics played a significant role in my life. I spent many years playing competitive soccer where I developed a love of fitness and learned the importance of hard work, resilience, and discipline. In high school, I was a member of Student Council and National Honor Society where I was privileged enough to have the opportunity to develop leadership skills while serving the school and community. I also played tennis and ran cross country.


My faith is central to who I am and has shaped many of my values and goals. I had the incredible opportunity of serving an 18-month mission in Guayaquil, Ecuador for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints. On my mission, I consecrated my time to God and serving his children. I saw lives change as I taught about the hope that comes through Jesus Christ. As I taught others, and experienced the happiness they exude, despite their difficult circumstances, they changed me. I learned one of the most important lessons of my life: joy is a choice you get to make everyday, and you can be happy no matter your circumstance.


I consider myself a driven individual with a strong work ethic. When I commit to something, I follow through and strive for excellence in all that I do. I enjoy taking initiative, leading others, and pursuing meaningful goals. Looking ahead, I aspire to build a successful business, create opportunities for others, and raise a strong family. I am motivated by continual growth and am committed to becoming the best version of myself in every area of life. I want to make a difference in this world and help others feel the love of God and rise to their full potential.
